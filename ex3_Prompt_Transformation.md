    Climate change refers to the long-term alteration of Earth’s climate, including shifts in temperature, rainfall patterns, and extreme weather events. While the climate naturally varies over time, recent changes are largely driven by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, which release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane (CH₄) into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat, causing the planet to warm—a phenomenon known as the **greenhouse effect**.

    **Key Impacts:**

    * **Melting ice and rising sea levels:** Glaciers and polar ice caps are shrinking, threatening coastal communities.
    * **Ecosystem disruption:** Animals and plants struggle to adapt, affecting biodiversity and food chains.
    * **Human consequences:** Climate change affects agriculture, health, water supply, and livelihoods.

    **Mitigation and Adaptation:**
    Efforts to combat climate change include reducing greenhouse gas emissions, transitioning to renewable energy, improving energy efficiency, protecting forests, and developing climate-resilient infrastructure.         **Climate Change Explained for High School Learners**

        Climate change is the long-term change in Earth’s weather and temperature. While the climate naturally changes over thousands of years, the problem today is that human activities are making the planet warm much faster. Burning fuels like coal, oil, and gas releases gases such as carbon dioxide and methane into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing the Earth to heat up—this is called the **greenhouse effect**.

        As a result, the world is experiencing higher temperatures, stronger storms, longer droughts, and rising sea levels. Ice at the poles and in glaciers is melting, and many animals and plants struggle to survive as their habitats change. Humans are also affected: crops may fail, water may become scarce, and extreme weather can damage homes and cities.

        Scientists suggest solutions called **mitigation** and **adaptation**. Mitigation means reducing the causes of climate change, like using renewable energy, planting trees, and wasting less energy. Adaptation means adjusting to the changes already happening, such as building flood defenses or planting crops that can survive heat.

        Everyone can help by conserving energy, recycling, and supporting clean energy. Understanding climate change is important because the actions we take today will determine the health of our planet for future generations.
