3. Compare the outputs and note how the tone changes while the content stays consistent.
    - Friendly Tone:
        The output email is friendly, warm and personal to the candidate. The first paragraph makes the candidate feel special and welcomed to the 
        interview because of words like "excited" and "Your experience caught our eyes". Also, the second paragraph make the candidate feels free
        and safe with the interview because words like " We promised it won't be all serious talk".
    
    - Professional Tone:
        The output is more formal, authoritative and proffessional. There was no room for making the candidate free and welcomed no words like " We promised it won't be all serious talk" rather We would like to invite you for a formal interview to discuss your qualifications and experience . The email was direct to the point of the purpose of the email without use of words that are friendley but authoritative.

    Despite the change of tone they both conveyed the same infortion at their various first paragraphs of inviting a candidate for an interview. Friendly Tone stated the invitation for an interview like "Your experience caught our eye, and we can’t wait to meet you in person and chat about how you could be part of our team." while Professional Tone invite a candidate for an interview directly as "We would like to invite you for a formal interview to discuss your qualifications and experience."
